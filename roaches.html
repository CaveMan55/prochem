Roaches

Roaches are incredibly resilient home invaders that carry with them deadly diseases and contaminants. Poor pest control not only fails to kill all of the roaches, but can make them resistant to further pest control treatments. In Fayetteville there are several things homeowners can do to limit their exposure to roaches and prevent an infestation.


  • Keep The House Clean--Roaches don't need a lot of food to survive, and when a house is full of garbage and debris, roaches will feast. It's especially important to clean around any area where food was prepared, stored or thrown away. Even the tiniest morsels can bring a flood of roaches.
  • Use Proper Food Storage--Roaches are drawn to open food containers, like a bag of chips or a loaf of bread. Homeowners should keep all open food items in sealed containers, and empty their refrigerator of leftovers or food remnants about once a week.
  • Use Baits And Sprays--Home pest control items, like sprays and roach motels, can head off an infestation before it gets out of hand. Fayetteville homeowners that keep the house clean and use proper food storage techniques can often stifle a full blown infestation if we kill the first wave of roaches that we see. Unfortunately, if the roaches continue to come back after the homeowner treats the site, it's time to call a professional.
  • Get Professional Help--Fayetteville is home to the American, German and Oriental cockroaches, each of which requires a different approach to treatment. American cockroaches love warm, moist environments, and treatment often means searching for leaks in the basement or sewer. German roaches prefer a slightly drier climate, and will burrow deep into the smallest holes or cracks in the home. Oriental roaches thrive in dark spaces, such as under the stairs, in closets or in the walls near the plumbing. All three varieties of roaches reproduce in only a matter of weeks, so it doesn't take long for a small roach problem to become a full-on infestation.
